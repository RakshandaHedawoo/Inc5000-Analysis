Inc 5000 Analysis¶

Inc 5000 (https://www.inc.com/inc5000) is a respected organization that annually compiles lists of high-growth companies.
The data file contains information about companies that were included in the Inc. 5000 list for the year 2018. These companies were ranked based on their percentage revenue growth from 2014 to 2017. To be eligible for inclusion, these companies needed to meet specific criteria:

  • They must have been established and generating revenue by March 31, 2014.
  • They must be privately held, for-profit entities based in the United States.
  • They should operate independently, not as subsidiaries or divisions of other companies, as of December 31, 2017. (Note that some of the companies on the list may have since gone public or been acquired.)
  • The minimum revenue required for 2014 was 100,000 dollars, and the minimum for 2017 was 2 million dollars.
  • Growth rates used to rank these companies were calculated with precision to two decimal places. In cases where multiple companies had the same growth rate, those with higher revenue were given a higher ranking.

DataSet Structure: inc 5000 company data¶

  1. Url (string) : Url link of inc 5000 website for each company located on inc 5000
  2. Rank (int) : Rank of the company according to the growth rate
  3. City (string) : City in which the company is located
  4. Growth (decimal) : Growth rate of the company
  5. Employees (int) : Number of employees in the company
  6. Company Name (string) : Company's name
  7. Website (string) : link for company's website
  8. City.1 (string) : State in which the company is located
  9. State (string) : State Code of the state in which the company is located
  10. Revenuw (decimal) : Revenue generated by the company
  11. Zip Code (string) : Zip code of the place where the company is located
  12. Founded (int) : Founding year of the company
  13. Industry (string) : Different industry to which the company belongs
  14. City,State (string) : City and State in which the company is located

  1.   2. Establishes firms started operating and generating revenue before 2014, so they will definitely generate a lot of revenue as compared to some startups that originated within the time period of 2013-2014. They will have low revenue as compared to these big firms.

  4. Also, the variability in the revenue could be big due to the various industries listed on the website. Each industry sector's revenue model is different.
  5. For example the revenue generated in the Food & Beverage industry will definitely vary with the revenue generated in Logistics and Transportation. As both the industries work in a very different way and both require different skill sets to work.

Let us see some examples of using a custom-made filtering function:¶

# we should list at least 20 rows
pd.set_option('min_rows',20)

# get's data based on a particular column name and a value for analysing column-wise data
def display_data(dataframe,number_of_rows=10,Category='None',value='None'):
    query_result = dataframe.sort_values(by='Revenue',ascending=False)
    if(Category!='None' and value!='None'):
        query_result = query_result[query_result[Category]==value]
    else:
        return query_result.iloc[:number_of_rows,:]
    return query_result.iloc[:number_of_rows,:]

Awards Distribution¶

Now that we have analysed some data column-wise, INC 5000 wants to present two awards to the Service-based companies listed in the top 50.

  1. Trailblazer Award : Given to the companies that are under top 25
  2. Pioneer Award : Given to the companies that didn't qualify for top 25, however are under top 50.

Firstly, we will add another column in our dataset called as 'Awards'.
Then we will assign the value of 'Trailblazer' to the top 25 companies and 'Pioneer' to the companies not in top 25 but in top 50.

Let us have a look at which companies got Trailblazers and which of them received Pioneer award¶

In [17]:
#Filtering companies by Service industry
service_based_company = inc_df[inc_df['Industry'].str.contains('Services')].sort_values(by='Revenue',ascending=False)
service_based_company.reset_index(inplace=True)

# additional Award column
service_based_company['Awards']='No Award'

# top quarter company count
top_quarter_count = int(service_based_company['Company Name'].count()*25/100)
# top half company count
top_half_count = int(service_based_company['Company Name'].count()*50/100)

# Trailblazer award to top quarter companies
service_based_company.loc[:top_quarter_count-1,'Awards'] = 'Trailblazer'

# Pioneer award to companies in top 50 but not in top 25
service_based_company.loc[top_quarter_count:top_half_count-1,'Awards'] = 'Pioneer'

service_based_company.loc[service_based_company['Awards']!='No Award',('Company Name','RANK','Revenue','Awards')]
